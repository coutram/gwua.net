Wood College of Education

The Wood School of Education has gained an international reputation of the diversity and excellence of its programs. The School continues its tradition of placing education first-exemplify through innovative teaching methods, cutting-edge research, and wide-reaching service.

The mission of the School of Education is to develop effective professionals and scholars informed by theory, research, and best practice. The mission is based on a strong belief in the value and critical importance of education in an open, democratic, and diverse society. The School recognizes that significant forces are reshaping our nation's schools and society. In response, we prepare and graduate professionals to meet, evaluate, and provide effective and creative leadership in response to these challenging times.

The School of Education offers graduate degrees in a variety of areas. Undergraduate degrees that may be earned are Associate of Science in Education and a Bachelor of Science in Education. The advanced degrees that may be earned are Master of Science, Educational Specialist, Doctor of Education and Doctor of Philosophy.

Graduate students supplement coursework by a variety of paraprofessional activities, such as assisting in research, teaching, and working in an education site under supervision. Research and teaching assistantships, fellowships and scholarships are available in limed numbers for highly qualified students.

Our graduate program prepare students for assuming productive roles in research, teaching, administration and program development in many areas. We encourage an interdisciplinary approach to research and scholarship. Our faculty collaborates with a diverse array of scholars and practitioners both with and outside the field of education.

Admission Requirements

All School of Education departments and programs have additional admission requirements, which may include letters of reference, resume, statement of interest, or a personal interview. Deadlines for submission of application materials vary by specific program area. For more information, consult the office of the Dean of Education.

Admission to all graduate programs in the School is selective and often times very competitive. Simply meeting the minimal criteria for admission does not ensure admission. To be admitted to one of the School's master's or the doctoral degree programs, students must complete the Online application for admission and arrange to have two official copies for their transcripts sent to the Office of Admission. Online applications for admission may be obtained here and must be returned by the deadline dates listed in the General Information section of the College catalog under Admission Information. Applicants are responsible for seeing that all documents are received by the Office of Admissions.

Courses taken as a post baccalaureate student do not apply toward a graduate degree except by special petition to the Dean of Education and with the approval of the student's advisor. A maximum of six credit hours or one semester/term completed as a post baccalaureate student may be considered for graduate credit.

Candidate's qualifications are assessed by review of their academic record, letters of recommendation, a personal statement, involvement in student affairs-related actives, and previous work experience. Individuals who appear to be qualified on the basis of submitted written materials will be invited to participate in an interview at their location or via telephone interview. All of the related materials must be received by the Director of Admissions prior to registration for courses. Evaluation criteria include the following:

  1. Graduate Studies: Potential for Success as an Educator

    Through the interview process and based on previous experiences and application materials, candidates must demonstrate that they possess good interpersonal skills, good oral and written communication skills, the ability to handle stresses inherent to student work, self-awareness, and sensitivity to individual difference, diversity, and social justice.

  2. Professional Goals

    Candidates must demonstrate a basic understanding of the student affairs field obtained though undergraduate student activities, volunteer work, paraprofessional or professional work experience, and informational interviewing. Candidates should have identified areas of interest within the student affairs field and a clear idea of what they hope to gain from graduate study. Processional goals, which understandably tentative, should be appropriate for beginning professionals and congruent with those of the School of Education.

  3. Academic Preparation and Ability

    The individual's junior-senior grade-point average must be at least 3.0 on a 4.0 system. The individual's overall grape-point average must be at least a 2.75 on a 4.0 system. Individuals who do not meet this requirement may request special consideration by submitting a letter indicating their rationale and evidence to support such consideration. This letter should also address in their goal statement the reasons for their undergraduate degree performance and discuss other academic indicators which provide supportive evidence of their potential to meet the demands of rigorous study at the graduate level.

    If a candidate has completed graduate coursework prior to application, his or her grade-point average for this coursework must be at least a 3.0 on a 4.0 system.

  4. International Applicants

    Applicants for who English is a second language should submit Test of English as a Foreign Language (TOEFL) scores as part of their application or successful complete a interview with the Dean of the School of Education or a professor of the School of Education. Please visit the TOEFL website, www.toefl.org, for information and application. Faculty of English, both in terms of understanding spoken and written English and in terms of communicating that understanding,, is a necessary prerequisite for graduate work in the School of Education. Because communication is integral to the student candidate must demonstrate written and oral communication skills.

    Should you have further questions about submitting your documents or applying as an international student, please contact the Dean of the School of Education.

Retention in Degree Program

To maintain their status in a degree program, students must be enrolled in consecutive college terms each calendar year, starting with the date of admission. Students who cannot register each semester must apply for a leave of absence or they will be dropped from the degree program. Students dropped for the degree program must petition and be approved by the Dean of the School of Education for reinstatement.

Transfer of Credits and Job Search

If you're planning to transferring schools, pursue additional study or working as an employer of the government, agency or an organization, be aware that each agency or organization has its own transfer or acceptance policy. Some organizations or agencies may accept your Hamilton College credits unconditionally. Some may not give you full credit. Some may reject your transcripts or degrees completely. If you want to make sure that your transcript and degree from Hamilton College will be accepted, make a list of potential transfer schools, agencies or organizations. Call each one and ask for a copy of their transfer and acceptance policy. No guarantee is given that credits or degrees will be accepted by any employer or governmental agency.
