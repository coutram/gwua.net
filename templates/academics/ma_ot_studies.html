Master of Arts in Old Testament Studies

Overview

While most of modern-day Evangelicalism points to the Bible as the basis of faith, there is a widespread tendency to treat the Old Testament like an inferior and irrelevant predecessor to the New. Many Christians rarely read it, and few preachers feel the need to preach from it. Yet the Old Testament was the “Bible” of the early church. And in it they found inspiration and a revelation of Jesus Christ and His will for their lives.

The MAOT degree is designed to give the student a broad background in understanding Old Testament biblical culture and history, biblical Hebrew and principles of interpretation, and a panoramic understanding of the whole of the Old Testament. It also provides students with a detailed analysis of contemporary theories of authorship and inspiration and equips them to defend the accuracy, authenticity and relevancy of the Old Testament in a skeptical world.

Each of the courses offered in the MAOT program attempts to immerse the student in the original texts so students have an understanding of how the Old Testament Scriptures were understood by their original audience as well as the early church. Students who successfully complete this program will be able to confidently, intelligently and accurately interpret the Old Testamenet and show its relevancy to contemporary culture.

Intended audience

The MAOT degree is designed for experienced ministers or Bible teachers who wish to increase their understanding of the original text of the Old Testament Scriptures so they are equipped to teach it in a way that is harmonious with its original intention. It is also suitable for serious laypeople who desire to deepen their understanding of the truths of the Old Testament as originally taught and embraced by the New Testament church.
