Medical Office Manager Training & Certification

The Basic Medical Office Manager Program is a 90 clock hour program in the fundamental aspects of medical office management. Unlike medical assisting training which takes up 6 months and covers areas that are not utilized in the medical office management occupation, this program is designed to get you working in as short a time as possible. The program is offered via distance learning and examinations are administered online. The basic course is geared toward individuals who are starting out as medical office managers.

This program is divided into three areas:

Area One (30 clock hours)

  • Introduction;
  • The Health Care Professional;
  • Personnel Management in the Medical Office;
  • The Front Medical Office;
  • Medical Records; and
  • Billing, Coding, and Collecting I.

Area Two (30 clock hours)

  • Billing, Coding, and Collecting II
  • Fraud, Abuse, and Compliance;
  • Documentation and Risk Assessment;
  • Ethical and Legal Issues;
  • Technology in the Health Care Environment; and
  • Employee Benefits.

Area Three (30 clock hours)

  • Health and Safety;
  • Responsibilities of the Office Manager;
  • Outpatient Services, Ambulatory, Surgical centers, and Hospitals;
  • The Business Side of Medicine; and
  • Medical Marketing.

Length of Program

Program completion is dependent on the student's ability to complete the sections and tasks assigned. Once all sections are completed, the student will be required to take a timed final exam. Students usually complete this program in 8 to 12 weeks.

Grade Requirements

Must be completed with a grade of "70%" or better

Admission Requirements

Prospective candidates for the Basic Medical Office Manager Training Program should have at least graduated from high school or earned a GED certificate. Prior experience in the medical office management field is not required. However applicants should have a strong desire to build a career in medical office management, be innovative, and possess strong reading, writing, math, and computer and reasoning skills.
