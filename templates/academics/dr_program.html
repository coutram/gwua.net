Doctoral Program Requirements

Georgetown Wesleyan University of the Americas (GWUA) requires that all doctoral programs reasonably conform to the specifications provided in the appropriate doctoral handbook below:

Doctor of Philosophy (PhD)
Doctor of Business Administration (DBA)
Doctor of Ministry (DMin)
Doctor of Education (EdD)

Forty-five credit hours are required for all doctoral programs. Five courses (15 credit hours) in the area of concentration may also be required. Each candidate will be evaluated on his or her prior education and life experiences to determine if additional credit hours in the area of concentration are required. A candidate and his or her Major Professor/ Faculty Advisor will also evaluate the candidate's additional requirements for the doctoral degree from the courses below. The GWUA Doctoral Requirements Worksheet will be completed by the candidate and faculty advisor (major professor) and forwarded to the office of the Provost for approval and assignment of the Doctoral Review Committee members. [Note: Students who do not have a master's degree in their area of proposed doctoral studies may be required to take up to 12 master's-level courses in the area of concentration before being admitted to doctoral-level courses.]

Core Courses (9-12 credits)

          DPP 700:  Orientation to Doctoral Program (3)*
          DPP 729: Writing Proposals (3)*
          DPP 739:  Directed Readings 1 (3)
          DPP 740:  Directed Readings 2 (3)

Select One of the Following (3 credits)

          DPP 733:  A Comprehensive Guide to Dissertation Content and Process (3)
          DPP 734:  A Comprehensive Guide to Practicum Content and Process (3)
          DPP 735:  A Comprehensive Guide to Project Content and Process (3)

Major Component Courses (select one group, 18-36 credits)

          DPP 755:  Dissertation  I (6-12)*
          DPP 756:  Dissertation II (6-12)
          DPP 757:  Dissertation III (6-12)

                                 or

          DPP 760:  Practicum I (6-12)*
          DPP 761:  Practicum II (6 -12)
          DPP 762:  Practicum III (6-12)
            
                                 or

          DPP 770:  Project I (6-12)*
          DPP 771:  Project II (6-12)
          DPP 772:  Project III (6-12)

*  Each course, I-III, is six months in length. A candidate may take up to one and a half years to complete the dissertation, practicum or project.  If additional time is required, the candidate may register  again for DPP 757, DPP 762 or DPP 772 each six months until the dissertation, practicum or project is completed and turned into the Office of the Provost. Additional credit will not be awarded.
